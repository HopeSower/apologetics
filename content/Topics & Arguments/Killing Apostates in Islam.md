# Killing Apostates in Islam: A Comprehensive Analysis

This page compiles classical Islamic texts from both Sunni and Shia traditions that mandate the death penalty for apostasy. Below you will find a summary of the key sources that form the foundation of the traditional ruling, along with links to detailed discussions on each narrative.

## 1. Sunni Sources on Apostasy

- **Sahih al-Bukhari**  
  - **Link:** [[Sahih al-Bukhari 3017]]  
  - **Link:** [[Sahih al-Bukhari 6922]]  
  - Link: [[Sunan an-Nasa'i 4059]]
  - Link: [[Sunan Ibn Majah 2535]]
  - Link: [[Sunan Abi Dawud 4351]]
  - **Summary:** Narrated by Ibn Abbas, this hadith records the Prophet’s explicit command: “Whoever changes his religion, kill him.”

- **Sahih Muslim**  & **Sunan an-Nasa'i**
  - **Link:** [[Sahih Muslim 1676a]]  
  - Link: [[Sunan an-Nasa'i 4017]]
  - Link: [[Sahih al-Bukhari 6878]]
  - Link: [[Hadith 14, 40 Hadith an-Nawawi]]
  - **Summary:** Reported by Abdullah bin Mas‘ud, this narration states that the Prophet forbade taking a Muslim’s life except in three cases—among them, apostasy. This categorization reinforces the view that apostasy is a capital offense.

- **Jami` at-Tirmidhi**  
  - **Link:** [[Jami` at-Tirmidhi 1458]]  
  - **Summary:** Also narrated by Ibn Abbas, this report reiterates the command, “Whoever changes his religion, then kill him,” with additional commentary noting that burning is not an acceptable substitute for execution.

- **Sunan Abi Dawud**  
  - **Link:** [[Sunan Abi Dawud 38_444]]  
  - **Summary:** This narration, again reported by Ibn Abbas, emphasizes that those who apostatize should be executed rather than subjected to alternative punishments like burning.

## 2. Shia Sources on Apostasy

- **Al-Kafi**  
  - **Link:** [[Al-Kafi 2_18_3]]  
  - **Summary:** In this narration, Imam Ja‘far al-Ṣādiq clearly states that any Muslim who renounces Islam must be executed without the possibility of repentance.

- **Man La Yahduruhu al-Faqih**  
  - **Link:** [[Man La Yahduruhu al-Faqih 3_65_7]]  
  - **Summary:** This narration, attributed to either Imam al-Ṣādiq or Imam al-Bāqir, reinforces that apostasy—especially when committed by a born-Muslim (murtad fitrī)—is punishable by death, with the apostate’s blood being deemed lawful for shedding.

- **Additional Shia Narrations**  
  - **Summary:** Other narrations found in sources such as *Tahdhīb al-Aḥkām* and statements from Imam Ali al-Riḍā further confirm that a born-Muslim who abandons the faith is to be executed without the option of repentance.

---

## 3. Comparative Analysis

Both Sunni and Shia sources present an unambiguous directive: apostasy is a capital offense. Despite modern reinterpretations aimed at softening these mandates, the original texts—spanning major hadith collections and doctrinal treatises—consistently affirm that the punishment for apostasy is execution.

---

## 4. Conclusion

The classical Islamic teaching on apostasy is clear and unequivocal: apostasy is punishable by death. This page has summarized the key narrations from both Sunni and Shia traditions that form the basis for this ruling.
