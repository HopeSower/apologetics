## Summary

Christians affirm both the full deity of Jesus and His genuine death on the cross. At first glance, these two truths seem contradictory—how can an eternal, unchanging, immortal God die? This article:

1. **States the Christian claim** clearly in biblical terms.  
2. **Explores the Islamic objection**, noting parallel concepts in Islamic theology.  
3. **Demonstrates why the Christian view is coherent** and why similar objections undermine Islamic doctrines.

---

## Key Points

- **Deity of Christ**: Jesus is the eternal Word (Logos), “with God” and “was God” ([[John 1_1]]).  
- **Incarnation**: The second Person of the Trinity took on human flesh ([[John 1_14]]; [[Philippians 2_5-11]]).  
- **Human Death**: By assuming a true human nature, Jesus could genuinely die “even death on a cross.”  
- **Islamic Objection**: “God cannot enter creation or die because creation is dependent and unworthy of worship.”  
- **Islamic Parallel**: In Islamic sources, Allah “descends” into creation nightly (Jamāʻat al-Ṣafar 446) and the Qurʾān has both an eternal and a created, physical nature.  
- **Consistent Rule**: If something eternal can become a temporal book and be burned (physical Qurʾān), it can likewise assume human flesh.  
- **Ultimate Contrast**: Islamic view limits God’s love and justice (e.g., “Allah does not love…” Surah 2:190, etc.), whereas Christian view portrays perfect love and justice: “While we were still sinners, Christ died for us” (Romans 5_6–8).

## 1. The Christian Position

### 1.1 Pre-Incarnate Deity  
> “In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God.” ([[John 1_1–3]])  
- **Eternally God** (Logos = divine essence).  
- **Distinct Persons**: “Word was with God” hints at Father–Son distinction.

### 1.2 The Incarnation  
> “The Word became flesh and dwelt among us.” ([[John 1_14]])  
- The **second Person** of the Trinity assumed a true human nature.  
- Retained full deity while adding genuine humanity.

### 1.3 Genuine Death  
> “He humbled himself by becoming obedient to death—even death on a cross.” ([[Philippians 2_5-11]])  
- **By nature**, God cannot die; **by assumption** of human nature, Jesus truly died.  
- Theology preserves both **immutability** of divine nature and **mortality** of human nature.

---

## 2. The Islamic Objection

### 2.1 Dependence and Worship  
> “Anything in creation is dependent and therefore unworthy of worship.”  
- Muslims argue: if Allah enters creation, He becomes dependent and thus not God.

### 2.2 Scriptural Constraints  
- Some Muslim scholars legislate that “God cannot take on a created form.”  
- Yet they accept ḥadīth of Allah descending nightly to the lowest heaven to hear prayers [Jami at-Tirmidhi 446](https://sunnah.com/tirmidhi:446) [Sahih Muslim 758e](https://sunnah.com/muslim:758e)[Sahih Muslim 758c](https://sunnah.com/muslim:758c) [Sunan Abi Dawud 1315](https://sunnah.com/abudawud:1315)[Sahih al-Bukhari 6321](https://sunnah.com/bukhari:6321)
- Yet in the Qurʾān itself, Allah enters into creation: in [[Quran 27_7–9]] He speaks from the fire to Moses, declaring “I am God, the Mighty, the Wise.”  

---

## 3. Islamic Parallel: Dual Natures

### 3.1 Qurʾān’s Eternal Speech vs. Physical Book  
- The Qurʾān is **uncreated**, eternal speech of Allah.  
- The **physical Qurʾān** (ink, paper, binding) is created and destructible.

### 3.2 Logical Consistency  
- If the divine Word can become a perishable book, why can’t it become a perishable human body?  
- Any rule forbidding the Incarnation equally forbids the dual-nature doctrine Muslims uphold for the Qurʾān.

## 5. The Christian Advantage

1. **Perfect Love**: God lays aside His glory to save His “children.”  
2. **Perfect Justice**: No sin goes unpunished; Christ bears penalty for believers.  
3. **Maximal Perfection**: Only the Christian God embodies perfect love, mercy, and justice.

---

## 6. Illustrative Analogy

> Suppose a king would sacrifice his royal robes and dive into mud to rescue his child.  
> —How much more would the eternal King of creation enter our world to save us!

## 7. Conclusion & Further Study

- Both Christians and Muslims affirm an eternal Word entering creation.  
- Only the Christian doctrine consistently allows that Word to die, by virtue of assuming true humanity.
